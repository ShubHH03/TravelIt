VICTORIA MEMORIAL

The Victoria Memorial, a huge monument made of white marble located in the heart of the City of Joy, Kolkata (erstwhile Calcutta) in West Bengal, India is one of the most famous monuments in West Bengal that has become a museum and popular tourist spot of the state. This grand and exquisite memorial not only stands as reminiscence to the rule of British Crown in the Indian subcontinent but also stands out as an excellent architectural gem in Indo-Saracenic revivalist style. It is a must visit tourist destination for first time visitors to experience the essence of Victorian era in the midst of the bustling metro city of Kolkata.

Between October to March
10 am - 5 pm

INDIAN MUSEUM

Regarded as one of the oldest museums in Asia, the Indian Museum was established back in 1814 and called Calcutta Indian Museum. To catch a glimpse of the illustrious history of Kolkata as a city and West Bengal as a state, you might want to opt for a visit here. The main reason why the museum is also recognised as a monument in Kolkata is because of its heritage status as an old museum. From Gandhara art to antiques from the Mohenjo Daro and Harappa civilizations, there are plenty of excellent exhibitions and antique collections that would certainly marvel you at the Indian Museum

HOWRAH BRIDGE

A lot has been said about the Howrah Bridge as one of the most iconic monuments in Kolkata. Better known as Rabindra Setu, the Howrah Bridge was actually constructed during the British era, by them, to ease trade and connect two parts of Kolkata. Perched atop the Hooghly River, the bridge is actually an excellent example of civil engineering. Along with a vehicle-based road, there is a specific pedestrian segment also built to enable people to walk through the bridge and reach the other side. Once again, it is the heritage nature of the bridge that gives it the title of a monument in Kolkata.

Best time to visit


Between October - February
10 am to 10 pm
